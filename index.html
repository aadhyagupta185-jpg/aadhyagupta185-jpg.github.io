I’m Aadhya, 22, and I think I see the world in textures before I see it in words — a handwoven scarf on a stranger, the way light falls across a crumpled bedsheet, the accidental gradient on a chai-stained napkin. Design, for me, is really just a way of paying attention. Outside the studio, I sing, collect colour swatches like other people collect playlists, and have a soft spot for late-night conversations that wander into philosophy. I believe beauty is a form of attention and that the best designs, like the best people, have a little wildness stitched into them.
